Firdaus Wong was born into a Chinese Buddhist family. Like many others, he grew up practicing the beliefs and traditions passed down through generations. For a long time, he followed Buddhism sincerely, respecting his culture and family background.
However, deep inside, Firdaus Wong felt a strong spiritual curiosity. He began asking big questions:
Who is the true Creator?
What is the real purpose of life?
Is God one, or many?
His search for truth led him to study different religions, including Christianity and Islam. While reading the Qur’an and learning about the concept of Tawheed (Oneness of God), he felt a clarity and peace he had never experienced before.
