### My Life Before Islam
Umair, a Brazilian ex-gangster, lived a "gore life" on the streets as a DJ and producer, deeply addicted to drugs, selling them, and fully immersed in crime. His tattoos symbolized street struggles, tears for lost partners, a mirror reflection for prison-like existence, and Mexico-inspired "thug life" gang affiliations. ### The Moment of Guidance
Constant danger heightened his instinctive faith, but recognizing life's fragility sparked deep reflection. WhyIslam materials—a free Quran, Prophet's history, and Islamic dos/don'ts—reborn him, filling his heart with peace through Allah's remembrance as in the Quran. ### Reflections After Islam
Islam liberated him from clubs, alcohol, cigarettes, and cocaine; now steadfast, he seeks to share dawah with street people trapped in paranoia and clouded minds. He calls for Portuguese translations to reach Brazil's underserved and credits WhyIslam's support.
