### **My Life Before Islam**
* **Interest in Religions:** Kaim began his journey by attending a school for religious studies. He was particularly interested in Abrahamic religions, though he initially knew almost nothing about Islam.
* **Illness and Transformation:** Before converting, he suffered from a serious illness that prompted him to re-evaluate his life and purpose, leading him to seek a deeper meaning for existence and the power governing the universe.
* **Traveling for Answers:** He traveled to Muslim countries to experience the reality of the faith firsthand, searching for the spiritual security he felt was missing in his life in Japan.

---

### **The Moment of Guidance**
* **The Impact of "Kitab al-Tawhid":** He was deeply moved by reading a book on *Tawhid* (Monotheism) in Japanese. The concept of God’s oneness and greatness touched his heart, and he began to realize that Islam is a complete way of life rather than just rituals.
* **The "Jugular Vein" Verse:** He was profoundly affected by the verse from Surah Qaf: **"And We are closer to him than [his] jugular vein."** He felt the awe of God's closeness and His knowledge of the soul's whispers, providing him with a simultaneous sense of security and reverence.
* **Taking Shahada at Al-Azhar:** Kaim decided to embrace Islam and took his *Shahada* (testimony of faith) at the Al-Azhar Mosque in Egypt. He described that moment as a rebirth and an indescribable feeling of peace.

---

### **Reflections**
* **Islam as a Way of Life:** Kaim believes that Islam is not merely about abstaining from forbidden things like alcohol and pork, but a comprehensive system regulating a person's relationship with their Creator and society.
* **Authorship and Outreach:** Just two years after his conversion, he authored a book titled "How to Live in Japan According to the Essence of Islam," aiming to guide Japanese people in understanding the core of the religion.
* **A Muslim Family:** He married a Muslim woman from Indonesia to lead a stable life based on Islamic principles, and he is dedicated to spreading Islamic culture within Japanese society.
* **Knowledge and Action:** He emphasizes that knowledge without action is useless. Therefore, he dedicates his time to translation and writing to bridge the information gap regarding Islam in Japan.
